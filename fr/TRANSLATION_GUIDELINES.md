## French translation guidelines

- Address the learner formally and consistently with "vous"; do not switch to "tu".
- Write clear, natural French for beginners. Prefer clear and precise explanations over literal translations, but do not add or omit technical meaning.
- Prefer natural gender-neutral words when referring to people; do not use parenthetical forms like "développeur(euse)". When needed, favor unwrapping gendered terms like "développeur ou développeuse".
- Keep code unchanged, including Godot/GDScript keywords, API names, identifiers, numeric values, strings, punctuation, and indentation.
- Preserve all BBCode tags and attributes: glossary terms, URLs, etc.
- Use consistent established French technical terms for programming and standard French punctuation, including spaces before ":", ";", "!", and "?".
